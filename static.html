Karen Gunderson
Approaching High Water

Approaching High Water exemplifies Gunderson’s skillful employment of a monochromatic palette of pure black to evoke the ebb and flow of waves and the play of light on the water’s surface. As viewers alter their positions, the reflections on the canvas shift, immersing them in the primordial movement of the sea. The result of two decades of study, this effect is achieved through precise brushwork and stencils that create ridges in the pigment that both cup and reflect light. The work’s title and the all-consuming tidal composition serve as a haunting reminder of the consequences of climate change.
